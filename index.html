What We Do

Not just accounting. Finance and operations — running as a system.

"Most finance outsourcing companies give you accountants. We give you a finance function."

"That means billing execution, agency filing, vendor controls, management reporting, and the dashboards your leadership actually needs — handled end-to-end by our team, delivered on a defined cadence, and built around how your specific business works."

01
Service Module
Revenue & Billing Operations

We manage your entire billing cycle from end to end. That includes pulling payments on the right cadence, tracking all private-pay and agency billing, identifying missed or underbilled charges, and reconciling what was billed against what was actually received.

For multi-location operators, we produce weekly recovery reports showing exactly what revenue was identified and collected that would otherwise have gone unnoticed.

What we explicitly handle:
  • Private-pay billing cycles (weekly, semi-monthly, monthly)
  • Agency billing filing and reconciliation
  • Unbilled hours tracking and escalation
  • Unauthorized adjustment identification and escalation tracks
  • Child-level or unit-level billing granularity tracking reports
  • AR aggressive follow-up and formal escalation protocols
02
Service Module
Expense & Vendor Controls

We build and maintain the controls that stop money leaking out of your business through vendor payments and operating expenses.

What we explicitly handle:
  • Automated and manual invoice intake from all vendor portals
  • Rigorous duplicate payment verification filters
  • Due-date tracking and consolidated payment scheduling
  • Royalty reconciliation against actual bank receipts
  • Continuous spend trend analytics and variance reviews
  • Month-end close cycles and consolidated expense verification
03
Service Module
Process Outsourcing & Operations Desk

We take over the operational back-office tasks that consume your team's time but don't require your team's judgment. Accounting is 10–20% of what we actually do. The rest is operations execution — the work that keeps your business running on time.

What we explicitly handle:
  • Direct portal access to corporate banking, vendor portals, and ERP systems
  • Complete billing execution pipelines handled end-to-end
  • Reimbursement validation and system processing gates
  • Overtime tracking metrics and daily/weekly labor reports
  • Weekly critical data requirements list published to the client
  • Defined escalation structures for any metric breaching normal parameters
04
Service Module
Financial Reporting & Business Intelligence

We deliver the financial and operational visibility your leadership needs to make decisions — not just a set of statements that confirm what happened last month.

What we explicitly deliver:
  • Comprehensive monthly financial packets — P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flows
  • C-Suite Executive Summaries with structural variance explanations
  • Custom Power BI dashboards mapping EBITDA by location and manager performance
  • Comparative center-vs-center performance matrix sheets
  • Enrollment mix and occupancy trends tracking revenue impact
  • Acquisition entity corporate reporting paths for M&A pipelines

Franchise Operators

You didn't open a franchise to spend your time on the books.

Your goal is straightforward — grow revenue, run profitable locations, and build something worth owning. Finance operations should support that. For most franchise owners, it gets in the way instead.

The Structural Multi-Unit Challenge

The franchise model is built for scale. The finance infrastructure rarely is.

When you buy a franchise, you get the brand, the model, and the training. You don't get a finance function. You build one — usually with whoever's available, using whatever system works for now.

That's fine at one location. At three it starts to crack. At ten it breaks. Multiple revenue streams, franchisor obligations, third-party programs — all running on different cycles. Most franchise owners know their numbers aren't clean. They just don't have the time to know by how much.

Scale Variance Comparison

If You Are Running One Location

You're tracking revenue yourself, or someone on your team is — after hours, usually manually. You know you're probably leaving money on the table somewhere but you can't find it. Every hour you spend on admin is an hour not spent on growth.

If You Are Running Multiple Locations

The problems you had at one location multiplied. One location's numbers are late. Another has an unresolved discrepancy nobody has chased. You have a report for everything and a real picture of nothing. The bigger you get, the further behind the finance function falls.

What Good Looks Like

  • You know, this week, which locations are up and which need attention — not six weeks later.
  • Every billing cycle runs completely and on time — nothing missed, nothing slipping through.
  • Revenue lost to missed charges and reconciliation gaps is identified and recovered every week.
  • Third-party program payments reconciled against what actually landed in the bank.
  • Billing, filing, and vendor payments run without your team touching them.
  • Month-end close on time, every month — with a clear explanation of what changed and why.

Accounting is about 10–20% of what we actually do. The rest is operations — the work that's been consuming your time and costing you revenue.
